# Demo Script

## Opening

Small businesses do not miss opportunities because they lack ambition. They miss them because the opportunity system is fragmented, confusing, and difficult to navigate.

AccessBridge AI is an AI-powered opportunity readiness platform that helps small businesses find, qualify for, and apply to grants, contracts, supplier diversity programs, and business funding.

## Demo Story

Today, we are using a commercial cleaning business in Riverside, California. The business wants to grow through contracts and funding but does not know where to start.

## Step 1: Business Profile

We start by entering basic business information: industry, location, years in business, goal, and available documents.

This gives the AI enough context to understand the business and begin matching it to relevant opportunities.

## Step 2: Opportunity Dashboard

AccessBridge AI returns matched opportunities with a match score, readiness score, deadline, and missing requirements.

This matters because finding an opportunity is only the first step. The real question is whether the business is ready to apply.

## Step 3: Opportunity Breakdown

The system explains why the business matches, where it is ready, and what is missing.

For example, the business may match a city janitorial contract but still need insurance, references, or a capability statement.

## Step 4: Generate Application Package

The AI generates a professional capability statement, proposal draft, and execution plan.

This turns confusion into a concrete path forward.

## Step 5: Enterprise Intelligence

For cities, nonprofits, and supplier diversity teams, AccessBridge AI aggregates readiness blockers across businesses.

Instead of guessing why businesses are not applying, institutions can see the actual barriers: missing insurance, lack of vendor registration, no capability statement, or weak documentation.

## Closing

AccessBridge AI is not just a grant finder. It is opportunity readiness infrastructure.

We help entrepreneurs access opportunities and help institutions distribute those opportunities more effectively.

Opportunity should not depend on insider knowledge.
